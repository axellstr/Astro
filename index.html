Frequently Asked Questions

We're partnering with a commercial space payload provider to secure a spot on a SpaceX Falcon 9 rideshare mission. Our Pepe figure will be housed in a CubeSat-sized container with a transparent viewport, allowing Pepe to "see" Earth from space. This approach is significantly more cost-effective than a dedicated launch and has been used by universities and small companies for similar projects.

A dedicated wallet containing 5% of the total token supply will be earmarked specifically for mission funding. As the token value increases, these funds will be used to finance payload development, secure the launch spot, and handle all logistical requirements. Additionally, we'll allocate 2% of all transaction fees to the mission wallet, ensuring continuous funding regardless of market conditions.

Community participation is central to our mission! Token holders will vote on key decisions including the final payload contents, Pepe's spacesuit design, and which messages from Earth will accompany Pepe to space. We'll also host regular AMAs with aerospace professionals working on our project, and the community will help design our mission patch that will be included in the payload.

Our payload will include a basic telemetry system that broadcasts location data. We're developing a dedicated tracking website where community members can see Pepe's orbital position in real-time, complete with simulated views of what Pepe might be seeing at that moment. For token holders, we'll also offer exclusive access to periodic actual imagery from the payload when it passes over partner ground stations.

Unlike vague promises of "going to the moon," our mission has a clearly defined, achievable goal with transparent milestones. Small satellites and payload missions to orbit happen weekly, and the costs are well-established. We're being fully transparent about the technical requirements, expected costs, and timeline. Additionally, we're assembling a team with actual aerospace experience to ensure the mission's success and will be publishing regular development updates with verifiable progress.
